# Analyzing EV Adoption: Mapping the Future of Clean Mobility

This project explores electric vehicle adoption through an interactive Tableau-based data visualization workflow focused on growth trends, charging infrastructure, emissions, air quality, and regional differences in clean mobility adoption.

The repository is built around the final Tableau workbook and polished presentation materials, with the strongest emphasis placed on the final report, interactive dashboard package, and presentation-ready assets.

## Project Focus

This project studies how EV adoption connects to:

- battery-electric and plug-in hybrid growth
- charging infrastructure availability
- regional and global adoption differences
- air quality and pollution trends
- CO2 emissions and sustainability indicators
- clean mobility outlook and future adoption patterns

The final deliverable is a Tableau story designed to present a multi-dimensional view of EV growth and environmental impact through maps, trend analysis, comparative dashboards, and policy-relevant visual narratives.

## Questions Explored

- How has global EV adoption changed over time?
- Which countries and regions are leading or lagging in EV growth?
- Does charging infrastructure appear to support higher EV adoption?
- How do EV adoption trends relate to air quality and emissions indicators?
- What regional patterns emerge when comparing adoption, infrastructure, and environmental metrics?

## Visualization Approach

The project uses Tableau to present:

- geospatial EV adoption maps
- time-series views of EV growth
- powertrain comparisons across BEV, PHEV, and other categories
- regional market share dashboards
- environmental trend comparisons
- dashboard storytelling for policy and sustainability analysis

## Main Takeaways

Based on the final report and presentation materials:

- EV adoption accelerated strongly after 2020
- BEVs showed stronger adoption momentum than other EV categories
- China and the EU were highlighted as major leaders in future EV adoption growth
- charging infrastructure and market growth were closely linked in the dashboard framing
- the project connects transportation transition with broader clean-mobility and environmental outcomes
